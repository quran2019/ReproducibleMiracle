5.8 Evidence with the Odd and the Even chapters at the Verse level

As per the rule, for each Al-Fatiha based evidence we have to test the even an the odd coding pattern over the same numbers used in that evidence. This chapter provides the even and odd evidence as a compulsory follow up to the Evidence 5.7 considering all the 6348 verses.

As per the rule, we separate all the verses into two categories as even and odd verses. If a verse has an odd chapter index then it is categorized as odd and otherwise as even. We then sum the chapter index, verse index and also the number of words of each verse and get their representation numbers. Since the operation is sum, the unnumbered verses has no effect at all in the representation numbers and thus we can use the special verse indexes while pairing. Then, we pair them from even to odd and concatenate all the verse level paired numbers and finally get the big representation number to be tested. As we will see in the following proof, this big number is multiple of 19. There is also and additional fact in this number. I said fact because in pairing I do not consider the reverse of the numbers as evidence since we are not attempting to rewrite the text. However, in this evidence the reverse of the pairing is also multiple of 19. This means, either we pair as usual or in the reverse order, both of the resulting big numbers are multiple of 19. This fact might suggest further assurance for the current evidence. Here is the proof:

#Get even and odd chapter verses
ev <- x[(x$chapter %% 2 ==0),]
cat("The number of even verses is",nrow(ev))

od <- x[(x$chapter %% 2 !=0),]
cat("The number of odd verses is",nrow(od))

x<- ev
